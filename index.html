1. "A" Sounds & Fundamental Blends

No. Vowel & Isolated Sound Trigger Pronunciation Tip (English) Example Sentence & Context Audio
1
a, à
Like "ah" in English. When written as "à", the pronunciation does not change.
Anne va à la plage. Anne goes to the beach.
2
â
Not always pronounced differently, but it sometimes makes the "a" sound a tiny bit longer.
Anne regarde son âne. Anne looks at her donkey.
3
ai
A and I combine to make an open "è" sound. Highly common in Imparfait past tense endings.
Anne aimait son âne. Anne loved her donkey.
4
au, eau
These letters combine to make a closed "o" sound, like the "o" in the English word "toe".
Au parc, Anne a de l'eau. At the park, Anne has water.

2. Variations of "E" (Accents & Silents)

No. Vowel & Isolated Sound Trigger Pronunciation Tip (English) Example Sentence & Context Audio
5
an, am...
Deep French nasal sound made from the back of the throat. Can also be spelled "en" or "em".
L'ambulance a un grand panneau blanc. The ambulance has a large white panel.
6
ain
A more smiling, flatter nasal sound. Perfectly identical to the "in" sound.
Anne a faim : elle mange du pain. Anne is hungry: she eats some bread.
7
e, eu
Sounds a bit like the "i" in "sir". Note that "e" is very frequently silent at the end of French words.
Je veux acheter ce jeu de cartes. I want to buy this card game.
8
oeu, eu
An open variant, sounding very similar to the "er" in the English word "her".
Ma soeur aime les fleurs. My sister loves flowers.
9
è, ê, e
An open "e" sound, identical to the vowel sound found in the English word "hair".
Cet hôtel avec la fête est près de la mer. This hotel with the party is close to the sea.
10
é, e
A crisp, closed sound like "ay" but spoken with a narrow mouth. Common for endings like "-er" and "-ez".
Éléonore et Anne sont allées à la plage l'été dernier. Eleonore and Anne went to the beach last summer.
11
en, em
Just another way to spell the exact same back-of-the-throat nasal sound as "an/am".
En avant, ensemble ! Forward, together!

3. Perfecting "I" & "O" Spaces

No. Vowel & Isolated Sound Trigger Pronunciation Tip (English) Example Sentence & Context Audio
12
i, y
Pronounced like a clean, sharp "ee" in "see". Keep your lips fully stretched back.
Il va y aller avec son ami Yves. He's going to go there with his friend Yves.
13
in, im...
The signature smiling French nasal sound. Pronounced identically to "ain".
Un matin, un lapin a pris un bain ? Impossible !! One morning, a rabbit took a bath? Impossible !!
14
o, ô
A pure, closed "o" sound. This matches the exact sound of the combination "au/eau".
Oh là là, l'hôtel Bonobo est très joli. Oh my, the Bonobo hotel is really pretty.
15
o
An open, short variant of "o", sounding closer to the vowel in "not" or "son".
Cet homme donne une corde a votre bonne. This man gives a rope to your maid.

4. Complex Combinations & Nasals

No. Vowel & Isolated Sound Trigger Pronunciation Tip (English) Example Sentence & Context Audio
16
on
A very rounded nasal sound formed directly on top of a closed "o" shape.
Son mouton a une toison très longue. His sheep has a very long coat.
17
ou
Pronounced similar to "oo" in English, but cleanly clipped without shifting your lips during the sound.
Où allez-vous ? Nous voulons aller à Tombouctou ! Where are you going? We want to go to Tombouctou!
18
oi
Pronounced like the sound string "wa" in English (e.g., "walk"). It is never read as "oy".
Toi ou moi? On décide à trois ! You or me? Let's decide on three!
19
oin
A fast compound nasal blend. It transitions quickly from a short "ou" to the smiling "in" sound.
J'ai besoin de moins de soin. I need less medical care.
20
u
Form a sharp English "ee" sound with your tongue forward, lock it, and push your lips outward in an "o" circle.
Tu as vu ? Lulu a conduit une voiture. Did you see? Lulu drove a car.
